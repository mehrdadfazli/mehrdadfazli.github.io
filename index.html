About Me

I am a PhD student in Computer Science at George Mason University, with a passion for AI and machine learning. I have worked as a research assistant, teaching assistant, and intern in both academic and industry settings. My research includes hallucination mitigation in vision-language models, epidemiological modeling, and network optimization. I’m always looking for opportunities to collaborate and learn.

Publications

  • Fazli, Mehrdad, et al. “Mitigating Hallucination in Large Vision-Language Models via Adaptive Attention Calibration.” arXiv:2505.21472 (2025)
    PDF Code
    This paper introduces CAAC, a decoding-time method that adaptively calibrates attention weights on image tokens to mitigate hallucinations in vision-language models. It requires no retraining or architectural change and achieves strong performance on multiple benchmarks.
  • Wei, Bowen, Mehrdad Fazli, et al. “Learning to Explain: Prototype-Based Surrogate Models for LLM Classification.” arXiv:2505.18970 (2025)
    PDF Code
    We propose a prototype-based surrogate model that explains black-box LLM predictions by identifying influential examples in training data. Our method improves interpretability without compromising performance.
